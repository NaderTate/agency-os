## Positioning

We build **real, code-backed AI systems** for local service businesses — receptionists that book and reschedule, voice agents that handle inbound and outbound, and automations that kill repetitive back-office work. Not no-code toys: the logic lives in code we own, so it does what the business actually needs.

**Ideal client:** an owner-operated local business that loses money to missed calls, no-shows, slow lead response, or manual scheduling. Dental, med spa, law, home services, auto, clinics.

## Pricing rules (for `/proposal`)

- Default to a **named tier** above. Only go custom when the scope clearly spans two tiers; then anchor to the nearest tier and itemize the delta.
- Always present **setup + monthly** separately so the recurring number is obvious.
- Include a one-line **ROI anchor** tied to their business (a recovered booking, a saved no-show, a faster lead response). Use example math, labeled as such — never invent their real numbers.
- The discovery call (service 1) is the default **next step** in every proposal unless the deal is already past that stage.

## Honest cost notes (so ROI math stays credible)

- Voice infrastructure runs ~$0.15–0.30/min all-in. A typical small business does a few hundred call-minutes/month → tens of dollars in cost against a $500–1,000/mo price.
- Speed-to-lead: responding in ~5 min vs ~30 min makes a contact far more likely to convert (MIT/InsideSales lead-response study). Use as the recurring-value argument.
